Hello! My name is Marisol Jimenez.

I'm a Design Engineering student at Brown University specializing in industrial design, with a special interest in user research, product development, and creating innovative physical and digital products.

My Portfolio

Student Success Dashboard

As part of my internship with Evisions, I co-created a Student Success Dashboard for universities, focusing on advising needs. My role involved market research, user testing, wireframing, and product roadmaps(along with other things), providing hands-on experience in product design and management.

Step Ladder

As part of the Brown University Design Brief course, my group researched, designed, and developed a multi-functional step stool, covering the entire product development cycle. We created a fully functioning prototype made from plywood and MDF, gaining hands-on experience in product design, prototyping, and material selection.

Drum Kit

As part of Brown University's Computer Aided Design and Visualization, we modeled a drum kit in SolidWorks We measured a physical kit, modeled parts, created components, and assembled the kit. Our work included CAM, motion analysis, and finite element analysis, emphasizing comprehensive modeling and analysis skills.

Portable Straw

For a CAD modeling project, I chose a portable straw with over five components. I modeled the parts and case, created an assembly, and produced engineering drawings for all parts and the final assembly, showcasing my proficiency in detailed CAD work and technical documentation.

Coffee Tumbler

In a CAD modeling assignment, I chose to model a coffee tumbler consisting of three components. I designed the individual parts, assembled them, and created detailed engineering drawings, highlighting my ability to handle simpler but precise CAD tasks and produce accurate technical documentation.
