Designing a Step Stool

The product development cycle: from research to prototype

Project Overview

Designed and developed a step stool from concept to fully functioning prototype, made from plywood and MDF, as part of a group project for Brown University's Design Brief course.

Role and Contributions

Took part in the design process, lead research, created CAD models, and built the final prototype.

Key Skills and Tools Used

  • CAD Modeling(Fusion 360)
  • Material Selection(Plywood and MDF)
  • Prototyping Techniques(low and high-fidelity)
  • Design Iteration and Testing

Design Process

Research

Conducted user interviews and market analysis to identify user need and existing solutions. Secondary research consisted of engineering consideration and current social and enviornmental impact of existing solutions.

Ideation

Brainstormed and sketched initial concepts, selecting the most viable and user-centric design for further development.

Design and Prototyping

Created detailed CAD models after creating low-fidelity prototypes. Eventually produced a fully-functioning prototyping.

Conclusion

Challenges and Solutions

Overcame material limitations by selecting suitable substitutes and iterated the design to enhance stability and usability.

Final Outcome

A robust and multi-functional step stool prototype made from plywood and MDF, featuring a slideing mechanism for storage.

Impact and Learnings

Learned the importance of iterative design and user feedback in creating a functional product. Received positive feedback from peers and intructors.
